## Join Our Gym. Our Family.

Our mission with Youth Martial Arts is to provide a character development program to the area's youth through martial arts. We want to provide children with a constructive outlet for their energy while gaining the attributes and qualities parents strive for in their children.

We specialize in strengthening students self-confidence, discipline, focus, leadership skills, respect for others, perseverance and integrity all while giving them an activity which will physically challenge their bodies and mentally stimulate their minds. This combination leads to a program which drives students to work hard and set goals not only in martial arts but in all walks of life.

For ages 5+ this class is meant to fulfill the active needs that youth have throughout their school years. We strive to have a Martial Arts class full of fun, friends and fitness! The classes are filled with physically challenging and mentally stimulating material that is catered to all ages of the youth program.

In Youth Martial Arts we use our Martial Arts as a means of enriching our lives and helping our bodies and minds grow. Because of this we do not use our Martial Arts to hurt other people. In Youth Martial Arts, we view all techniques that the youth learn as tools, not weapons. The students are rewarded for their efforts via the belt ranking system where the children can achieve the rank of black belt.

## Program Benefits

Youth Martial Arts includes more than just Martial Arts. We include a Merit Badge system and provide a well rounded Character Development Program.

### Merit Badges

Our Merit Badge Program allows for each Youth Martial Artist to develop the life skills they will need to succeed, not only in the classroom, but in the world around them. Through quick daily discussions about topics such as; respect, manners, determination, and focus, students are able to listen to their peers and their instructors share stories that reinforce what being a loving, caring, and productive member of society really means. Each badge is discussed in class, then accompanied by a task sheet that the parent can help the child fill out and sign-off on. The child then presents their accomplishments in front of the class and is awarded a Merit Badge which can be ironed onto their uniform.

### Physical Fitness

While our Youth Martial Arts program has a significant focus on character development and life skills, we also pride ourselves on stellar martial arts instruction and physical fitness. Each class includes cardio-conditioning, mat movements and other core-strengthening exercises, technical review sessions, and partner drilling and padwork. CPAMMA offers a true all-inclusive Youth Martial Arts experience.

### Character Development

Through partner drills and padwork, the Merit Badge Program, and individual and team games, your child will learn the importance of working well with others and helping one another to succeed. In each class your child will learn to help guide the younger students, while also being challenged and motivated by the older students.

## Classes & Programs (Detailed)

### Women's Kickboxing

Get a workout like never before in this positive, upbeat class. Whether you are interested in losing weight, toning muscle, or becoming a competitive athlete, this class is for you! Classes include quality technique instruction, intense kickboxing drills, high-cardio partner drills, and plenty of floor conditioning and core exercise. Our Women's Kickboxing Program is taught by certified muay thai instructors and thai boxing champions, all in a fun, laid-back atmosphere.

Classes are made to burn calories, lose weight, increase strength, and pave the way to a happier, healthier YOU! Experience what the hype is all about!

### Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

Made famous by the Gracie family, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) is a martial art based on grappling and ground fighting techniques. The aim of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is to control and subdue a larger and stronger opponent through the use of superior techniques, leverage, and body mechanics. With an emphasis on technical grappling rather than striking, students are able to practice with varying levels of intensity, decreasing the threat of serious injuries which can sometimes occur in other martial arts.

We train with progressive resistance in a fun, relaxed, and friendly environment. You will learn takedowns, pin escapes, sweeps (reversals), chokes, joint locks, and much more! Whether your goals are self-defense, competition, or just to have fun, there is a place for you at our gym!

### Muay Thai & Boxing

Our Striking program consists of two main influences; Muay Thai & Boxing. Muay Thai Kick Boxing; the art and science of eight limbs, is quickly being recognized as the most complete and beneficial striking art for the modern striker. Muay Thai, translated into English as Thai Boxing, is the national sport of Thailand and is a martial art with origins in the ancient battlefield tactics of the Siamese army. Thai training methods develop devastating power, insurmountable speed, and superb cardiovascular endurance.

This class is for people interested specifically in stand-up fighting, or for those looking to compliment their ground game. Striking classes are structured with a cardio warm-up, 30 minutes of technique and drilling taught by our world-class certified Muay Thai Instructors, and 30 minutes of high-intensity rounds ranging from pad work, to heavy-bag work, to sparring.

Ajarn Ryan Gruhn has led a number of fighters into the ring and onto successful and winning careers in Muay Thai. Whether you are looking to compete, or looking for a great work-out, this is the place for you!

### Youth Martial Arts

Our mission with Youth Martial Arts is to provide a character development program to the area's youth through martial arts. We want to provide children with a constructive outlet for their energy while gaining the attributes and qualities parents strive for in their children.

We specialize in strengthening students self confidence, discipline, focus, leadership skills, respect for others, perseverance and integrity all while giving them an activity which will physically challenge their bodies and mentally stimulate their minds. This combination leads to a program which drives students to work hard and set goals not only in martial arts but in all walks of life.

For ages 5+ this class is meant to fulfill the active needs that youth have throughout their school years. We strive to have a Martial Arts class full of fun, friends and fitness! The classes are filled with physically challenging and mentally stimulating material that is catered to all ages of the youth program.

In Youth Martial Arts we use our Martial Arts as a means of enriching our lives and helping our bodies and minds grow. Because of this we do not use our Martial Arts to hurt other people. In Youth Martial Arts, we view all techniques that the youth learn as tools, not weapons. The students are rewarded for their efforts via the belt ranking system where the children can achieve the rank of black belt.

### Reality Based MMA

Reality Based Mixed Martial Arts focuses on the arts and techniques known to be successful in resolving real life situations. Rather than focusing on Sport Fighting (one on one), RBMMA looks at fighting when there are no rules which can lead to multiple attackers, weapons and many other variables. As such, RBMMA trains students to learn the difference between sport oriented techniques and survival oriented techniques. This class is for the person interested in the sport side of Mixed Martial Arts but training it for real life situations.

RBMMA pulls influences from all of the arts inside MMA (Muay Thai, Boxing, Wrestling, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu) but also gives credit to the Filipino Martial Arts, Krabi-Krabong, Burmese Boxing, Silat and other weapon oriented arts. Additionally, in most real life situations weight classes are not an option. We make sure to train as such where everyone trains with everyone regardless of size or skill level.

This class is taught by Guro Ryan "Guard Dog" Gruhn; a Dog Brothers Martial Arts and in Filipino Martial Arts (Kali / Escrima / Silat / Arnis) under the Inosanto lineage. Guro Guard Dog has fought at multiple Gatherings and has ended a number of his fights in multiple organizations by submission, KO and TKO.

### Mixed Martial Arts

Our Mixed Martial Arts class synergizes the four key elements of today's popular "Mixed Martial Arts" as seen in the UFC. These four elements are Boxing, Muay Thai, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, and Wrestling.

While other local programs clump all of these areas into one class only a few days a week, we like to ensure you are getting top level instruction in each area by instructors who specialize in each discipline. Students in the MMA program take classes in Muay Thai, Boxing, Jiu-Jitsu, and Wrestling, in order to really be able to hone-in on each skill set. This ultimately builds fighters who are well rounded, and avoids the "Jack of all trades, master of none" paradox.

### Weight Loss

Our programs have become a proven way of losing weight and getting in shape through fun, upbeat classes.

Striking classes consist of a dynamic warm up, partner drills, along with a bag and pad workout like no other! 30-45 minutes of cardio where you set your own pace followed by abs after every class!

### Cardio

All of our adult classes include 30-60 minutes of cardio set at the student's pace. If you are a beginner, the workouts allow you to set a comfortable pace while experienced athletes can push themselves for the ultimate workout.

### Strength Training

Our programs have become second to none in increasing strength and lean muscle, while maintaining a high energy, fun environment! Through repetitive punching and kicking in our striking classes, our students develop significant strength in their shoulders, arms, chest, legs, hips and buttocks.

Our Jiu-Jitsu program also offers an all-over strength make-over, focusing primarily on the core muscle groups. Through continuous give-and-take of body weight, transitions between positions, and resistance between holds and submissions, your stomach, chest, and back go through a strength transformation like no other.

### Flexibility

As we get older, decreases in flexibility can often make even day-to-day activities seem unnecessarily taxing. Our programs at CPAMMA will increase flexibility in all fitness levels. Each class is structured with a warm-up tailored to loosen joints and relax muscles. Students start at a level that is most complimentary to their flexibility level, and progress only when they are ready.

## What do I need to bring to my first class?

**Striking Arts** – We will supply you with a pair of gloves for your free trial class or you can bring your own. You can also sign up in advance and purchase gloves at a discounted rate. Any closed glove that doesn't expose fingers or palm will work. Avoid gloves from Walmart or Dick's Sporting Goods due to reported student injuries. Purchase appropriate gloves at CPAMMA.

**Grappling Arts** – We will supply you with a gi to try out a class or you can sign up beforehand and purchase a gi for the special rate.

**Clothing** – Simply wear something comfortable to move around in. Shorts and a t-shirt work fine. No shoes on mats; bare feet or socks preferred. For Jiu-Jitsu classes, you'll need to purchase a gi. CPAMMA provides gis for free trial classes.

## Class Format Policies

### ADULT CLASSES

#### Attendance & Class Format

During slower seasons, auxiliary time slots may have 3 or fewer students. In the case of 3 or less students the format for a class will be "open." For striking, classes will hit pads. For grappling, "Open Roll" format applies. An instructor will lead these classes but no new material will be presented. Students should use these sessions to practice learned techniques and ask specific questions.

#### Open Mat / Low Attendance

Instructors will wait 15 minutes in the event that there are no attendees for a class/open mat. After 15 minutes without students, instructors may cancel the class. Please make sure that you are at the class/open mat on time. This rarely occurs but becomes possible during bad weather or breaks.

#### Certified Instructors

Each class will be run by a certified instructor or a certified assistant in the particular art of the class. CPAMMA ensures each student trains with at least two different instructors.

### YOUTH CLASSES

#### Certified Instructors

In the case of Youth Martial Arts there will be a certified Black Belt Instructor (Sifu Ryan for example) in at least one of the classes for each child per week.

#### Age Diversity

Mighty Mites is age specific (3 & 4 year olds) but all Youth Martial Arts classes include children ages 5-12 years old. Adult/Teen programs may include younger students. Having such age diversity allows students to become comfortable with students of a different age along with having training partners of all sizes.

#### Number of Classes

All Youth Martial Arts students sign up for one main class per week and one auxiliary class per week. The $99 payment covers four monthly classes. However, we offer the secondary auxiliary class each week to allow students to make up a class in the event that they miss their main class time slot.

## Competitive Requirements

The gym's philosophy emphasizes winning over rushing to compete. Our goal is for our students to *win* fights. Previous students have become successful professionals in Strikeforce, UFC, Golden Gloves boxing, and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu competitions.

## I don't have any experience, is that okay?

Yes! The gym accepts new students with or without experience. We will place you with an assistant instructor to go over the fundamental material needed to join the group. Attend your preferred class at its normal time and proper placement follows.

## What is the difference between striking and women's kickboxing?

The only difference between Striking (Muay Thai & Boxing) and Women's Kickboxing is that Women's Kickboxing is "women only" and Striking is "Co-Ed." The co-ed classes maintain approximately 60/40 male/female split. Both classes will give you a great cardio workout that is built on the fundamentals of Muay Thai. Competitive opportunities exist for interested participants.

## I will be missing several classes. Can I take some other classes in replacement?

YES! Members can add programs to compensate for missed classes at additional cost. We cannot however allow someone to mix and match classes from different programs to fit their schedule. Each program spot is reserved for paying students of that specific program.

## When do I have to sign up by? When does the next session start?

We offer ongoing enrollment which means that you may start at any time during the year. Online learning is also available to aid material absorption.

## I want to compete, how long will it take me until I'll have my first fight?

Readiness depends on training dedication and individual learning pace. Our current fighters who are serious about their training and competitive career take all of the classes. Without serious commitment, progress takes longer. It should be every "fighter's" goal to "get good first" before they even think about fighting.

## Do you teach UFC at your gym?

UFC is an organization, it is not a style, nor is it a class. For UFC-associated arts, the Mixed Martial Arts program is recommended.

## I don't want to take classes. Can I just pay for a gym membership and come in and use the bags and equipment?

To use the Martial Arts facilities of Central PA Mixed Martial Arts, you need to be a member of one of the programs.

## Where can I park?

You can park in the lot directly outside of the gym for FREE!

## Classes & Programs

##### Women's Kickboxing

Get a workout like never before in this positive, upbeat class. Whether you are interested in losing weight, toning muscle, or becoming a competitive athlete, this class is for you! Classes include quality technique instruction, intense kickboxing drills, high-cardio partner drills, and plenty of floor conditioning and core exercise. Our Women's Kickboxing Program is taught by certified muay thai instructors and thai boxing champions, all in a fun, laid-back atmosphere.

Classes are made to burn calories, lose weight, increase strength, and pave the way to a happier, healthier YOU! Experience what the hype is all about!

##### Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

Made famous by the Gracie family, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) is a martial art based on grappling and ground fighting techniques. The aim of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is to control and subdue a larger and stronger opponent through the use of superior techniques, leverage, and body mechanics. With an emphasis on technical grappling rather than striking, students are able to practice with varying levels of intensity, decreasing the threat of serious injuries which can sometimes occur in other martial arts.

We train with progressive resistance in a fun, relaxed, and friendly environment. You will learn takedowns, pin escapes, sweeps (reversals), chokes, joint locks, and much more! Whether your goals are self-defense, competition, or just to have fun, there is a place for you at our gym!

##### Muay Thai & Boxing

Our Striking program consists of two main influences; Muay Thai & Boxing.  Muay Thai Kick Boxing; the art and science of eight limbs, is quickly being recognized as the most complete and beneficial striking art for the modern striker.  Muay Thai, translated into English as Thai Boxing, is the national sport of Thailand and is a martial art with origins in the ancient battlefield tactics of the Siamese army.  Thai training methods develop devastating power, insurmountable speed, and superb cardiovascular endurance.

This class is for people interested specifically in stand-up fighting, or for those looking to compliment their ground game.  Striking classes are structured with a cardio warm-up, 30 minutes of technique and drilling taught by our world-class certified Muay Thai Instructors, and 30 minutes of high-intensity rounds ranging from pad work, to heavy-bag work, to sparring.

Ajarn Ryan Gruhn has led a number of fighters into the ring and onto successful and winning careers in Muay Thai. Whether you are looking to compete, or looking for a great work-out, this is the place for you!

##### Youth Martial Arts

Our mission with Youth Martial Arts is to provide a character development program to the area's youth through martial arts. We want to provide children with a constructive outlet for their energy while gaining the attributes and qualities parents strive for in their children.

We specialize in strengthening students self confidence, discipline, focus, leadership skills, respect for others, perseverance and integrity all while giving them an activity which will physically challenge their bodies and mentally stimulate their minds. This combination leads to a program which drives students to work hard and set goals not only in martial arts but in all walks of life.

For ages 5+ this class is meant to fulfill the active needs that youth have throughout their school years. We strive to have a Martial Arts class full of fun, friends and fitness! The classes are filled with physically challenging and mentally stimulating material that is catered to all ages of the youth program.

In Youth Martial Arts we use our Martial Arts as a means of enriching our lives and helping our bodies and minds grow. Because of this we do not use our Martial Arts to hurt other people. In Youth Martial Arts, we view all techniques that the youth learn as tools, not weapons. The students are rewarded for their efforts via the belt ranking system where the children can achieve the rank of black belt.

##### Reality Based MMA

Reality Based Mixed Martial Arts focuses on the arts and techniques known to be successful in resolving real life situations. Rather than focusing on Sport Fighting (one on one), RBMMA looks at fighting when there are no rules which can lead to multiple attackers, weapons and many other variables. As such, RBMMA trains students to learn the difference between sport oriented techniques and survival oriented techniques. This class is for the person interested in the sport side of Mixed Martial Arts but training it for real life situations.

RBMMA pulls influences from all of the arts inside MMA (Muay Thai, Boxing, Wrestling, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu) but also gives credit to the Filipino Martial Arts, Krabi-Krabong, Burmese Boxing, Silat and other weapon oriented arts. Additionally, in most real life situations weight classes are not an option. We make sure to train as such where everyone trains with everyone regardless of size or skill level.

This class is taught by Guro Ryan "Guard Dog" Gruhn; a Dog Brothers Martial Arts and in Filipino Martial Arts (Kali / Escrima / Silat / Arnis) under the Inosanto lineage. Guro Guard Dog has fought at multiple Gatherings and has ended a number of his fights in multiple organizations by submission, KO and TKO.

##### Mixed Martial Arts

Our Mixed Martial Arts class synergizes the four key elements of today's popular "Mixed Martial Arts" as seen in the UFC. These four elements are Boxing, Muay Thai, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, and Wrestling.

While other local programs clump all of these areas into one class only a few days a week, we like to ensure you are getting top level instruction in each area by instructors who specialize in each discipline. Students in the MMA program take classes in Muay Thai, Boxing, Jiu-Jitsu, and Wrestling, in order to really be able to hone-in on each skill set. This ultimately builds fighters who are well rounded, and avoids the "Jack of all trades, master of none" paradox.

##### Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

Made famous by the Gracie family, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) is a martial art based on grappling and ground fighting techniques. The aim of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is to control and subdue a larger and stronger opponent through the use of superior techniques, leverage, and body mechanics. With an emphasis on technical grappling rather than striking, students are able to practice with varying levels of intensity, decreasing the threat of serious injuries which can sometimes occur in other martial arts.

We train with progressive resistance in a fun, relaxed, and friendly environment. You will learn takedowns, pin escapes, sweeps (reversals), chokes, joint locks, and much more! Whether your goals are self-defense, competition, or just to have fun, there is a place for you at our gym!

##### Muay Thai & Boxing

Our Striking program consists of two main influences; Muay Thai & Boxing.  Muay Thai Kick Boxing; the art and science of eight limbs, is quickly being recognized as the most complete and beneficial striking art for the modern striker.  Muay Thai, translated into English as Thai Boxing, is the national sport of Thailand and is a martial art with origins in the ancient battlefield tactics of the Siamese army.  Thai training methods develop devastating power, insurmountable speed, and superb cardiovascular endurance.

This class is for people interested specifically in stand-up fighting, or for those looking to compliment their ground game.  Striking classes are structured with a cardio warm-up, 30 minutes of technique and drilling taught by our world-class certified Muay Thai Instructors, and 30 minutes of high-intensity rounds ranging from pad work, to heavy-bag work, to sparring.

Ajarn Ryan Gruhn has led a number of fighters into the ring and onto successful and winning careers in Muay Thai. Whether you are looking to compete, or looking for a great work-out, this is the place for you!

##### Reality Based MMA

Reality Based Mixed Martial Arts focuses on the arts and techniques known to be successful in resolving real life situations. Rather than focusing on Sport Fighting (one on one), RBMMA looks at fighting when there are no rules which can lead to multiple attackers, weapons and many other variables. As such, RBMMA trains students to learn the difference between sport oriented techniques and survival oriented techniques. This class is for the person interested in the sport side of Mixed Martial Arts but training it for real life situations.

RBMMA pulls influences from all of the arts inside MMA (Muay Thai, Boxing, Wrestling, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu) but also gives credit to the Filipino Martial Arts, Krabi-Krabong, Burmese Boxing, Silat and other weapon oriented arts. Additionally, in most real life situations weight classes are not an option. We make sure to train as such where everyone trains with everyone regardless of size or skill level.

This class is taught by Guro Ryan "Guard Dog" Gruhn; a Dog Brothers Martial Arts and in Filipino Martial Arts (Kali / Escrima / Silat / Arnis) under the Inosanto lineage. Guro Guard Dog has fought at multiple Gatherings and has ended a number of his fights in multiple organizations by submission, KO and TKO.

##### What do I need to bring to my first class?

_Striking Arts_ – We will supply you with a pair **OF GLOVES** for your free trial class or you can bring your own.  You can also sign up before your first class and purchase gloves for a discounted rate because you signed up in advance.  Any glove that is closed and does not expose the fingers or palm will be fine.  If you are purchasing your own please avoid any gloves sold at Wal-Mart or Dick's Sporting Goods as we have had students report injuries from them,  Appropriate gloves can be purchased at CPAMMA.

Grappling Arts - We will supply you with a gi to try out a class or you can sign up before hand and purchase a gi for the special rate we have running for that particular day.

Clothing - Simply wear something comfortable to move around in. Shorts and a t-shirt for example will do just fine.  We do not wear shoes on our mat so bare feet or socks is preferred.  For Jiu-Jitsu classes you will need to purchase a gi.  We will provide gis for your free trial class.

##### Class Format Policies

## ADULT CLASSES

#### Attendance & Class Format:

During the slower times of the year there is a chance for our auxiliary time slots to have smaller numbers.  In the case of 3 or less students the format for a class will be "open."  For striking classes the class will hit pads.  For Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, Submission Wrestling and Wrestling the format will be "Open Roll."  An instructor will lead these classes but no new material will be presented. We recommend using such time slots to work on techniques you have already learned and to ask instructors specific questions relating to your training.  Such classes can be highly valuable to the students who attend!

#### Open Mat / Low Attendance:

Instructors will wait 15 minutes in the event that there are no attendees for a class/open mat.  In the event that after 15 minutes there are no students the instructor reserves the right to cancel the class/open mat. Please make sure that you are at the class/open mat on time. While this rarely happens, it does become possible when bad weather is imminent as people choose not to travel. This also becomes a possibility during breaks or holidays so please plan accordingly.

#### Certified Instructors:

Each class will be run by a certified instructor or a certified assistant in the particular art of the class.  One of the great qualities of CPAMMA is the expansive list of instructors that all students have access to.  We make a point to make sure each student sees at least two different instructors in their training.

## YOUTH CLASSES

#### Certified Instructors :

In the case of Youth Martial Arts there will be a certified Black Belt Instructor (Sifu Ryan for example) in at least one of the classes for each child per week. It is important to our head instructors that they see each instructor at least once per week.  In some instances there is the chance that a child may be with another instructor/assistants for more than one class in a row.

Mighty Mites is age specific (3 & 4 year olds) but all Youth Martial Arts classes include children ages 5-12 years old.  Adult/Teen programs may also have students who are of a younger age. All Youth Martial Arts students sign up for one main class per week and one auxiliary class per week.  The $99 payment is for four classes a month. We encourage parents to have their child(ren) come to both classes each week as often as possible to make up for times that a child cannot make a class due to something outside of their control.

##### Sparring Requirements

## SPARRING REQUIREMENTS

Below are our guidelines on when students are allowed to spar with other students.  Following this guide will insure CPAMMA that the students who are sparring with strikes have had enough time to understand the art before they play the game that is sparring.  Sparring is a privilege in any Martial Arts and should be treated as such.

**BRAZILIAN JIU-JITSU / SUBMISSION WRESTLING:**

_Sparring allowed from Day 1 (with approval) with instructor present until first stripe_

**STRIKING:**

_- Yellow Arm Band can situational spar with Red Arm Band & Above_
_- Orange Arm Band can situational spar with Purple Arm Band & Above_
_- Green Arm Band can spar with Green Arm Band & Above_

**MIXED MARTIAL ARTS:**

_- Stand Up Sparring follows "Striking Requirements"_
_- Grappling Sparring follows "BJJ/SW Requirements"_
_- Striking on the Ground Sparring for Green Arm Bands & Above_

**REALITY BASED MMA (FILIPINO MARTIAL ARTS):**

_- Stick sparring allowed from Day 1 with instructor present (with approval)_
_- Full RBMMA sparring with striking and grappling allowed when instructor gives approval_

##### I want to compete, how long will it take me until I'll have my first fight?

Being ready to compete is largely dependent on how much time you can dedicate to your training. In addition, please understand that everyone learns at different speeds. Our current fighters who are serious about their training and competitive carrier take all of the classes.  If you are unable to commit to a serious training schedule it will take you longer than someone who is willing to put in the hours of training to get good.  With that said, it should be every "fighter's" goal to "get good first" before they even think about fighting.
